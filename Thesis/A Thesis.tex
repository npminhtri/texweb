\begin{abstract} \doublespacing
  \addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
 In this thesis, we study the notion of semi-stability of lattices in finite dimensional vector space of dimension at least 3.

 Semi-stable lattices can be defined using two different definitions: using a geometric plot as in \cite{MR780079} or Lie theoretically the using of Harish-Chandra map as in \cite{MR3969872}. We show that 
 the two definition are equivalent and prove the equivalence between geometric properties using plot and algebraic properties using Harish-Chandra map. 
 
  
 When the lattice is not semi-stable, we call it unstable, and attach 
 a canonical filtration in geometric sense and a  canonical pair in algebraic sense. We also prove that these objects encode essentially the same information.
